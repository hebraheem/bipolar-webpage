Take assesment to help us know you

You’ve identified symptoms commonly associated with bipolar disorder. People struggling with bipolar disorder experience mood fluctuations between highs and lows. During a manic episode they feel excessively energetic, irritated, or outgoing. They also struggle with racing thoughts that are very difficult to follow, and they might even feel like they are indestructible or “larger than life.”
This disorder greatly interferes with people’s lives. Below is a detailed list of symptoms that many people experience when they’re struggling with bipolar disorder.

Lacking the desire to sleep or the need for normal amounts of sleep

Talking in excessive amount

Having an exaggerated sense of self-worth

Experiencing an excessive amount of racing thoughts

Becoming easily distracted

Taking on an excessive workload or number of projects

Engaging in exciting activities that are also potentially dangerous, like spending too much money on unnecessary items, driving too fast, or engaging in anonymous sexual encounters

Feeling sad, disappointed, unhappy, or hopeless nearly every day for at least a few weeks, or comments by other people that you frequently look this way

Lack of interest in normal, pleasurable activities

Unexpected increase or decrease in appetite and/or weight

Inability to sleep or sleeping more than usual

Excessive sluggishness and inactivity

Feeling valueless, hollow, or guilty

Recurring thoughts about death or suicide
